# ADR-007: Leverage Tiers & Alpha Stack V2

**Status:** Approved (2026-04-19)  
**Context:** Paper Trading setup, cost coverage required  
**Decision:** Asset-specific static leverage tiers + Alpha Stack V2 roadmap

## Leverage Tiers (Static)

| Tier | Leverage | Assets | DD Range | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1.8x | BTC, ETH | 18.3-18.5% | Low volatility, deepest liquidity |
| 2 | 1.5x | SOL, LINK, ADA | 17.9-19.9% | Medium volatility |
| 3 | 1.0x | AVAX | 18.3% | High volatility, conservative |

**Why static, not dynamic:**  
Dynamic auto-derate (reduce leverage on guard trigger) adds state complexity 
to the State Manager. For V1, static tiers are robust, predictable, and 
sufficient. Dynamic leverage is a V2+ consideration.

**Why not 3x:**  
DD at 3x = 29-51%. KILL switch fires constantly. Mathematically untenable.

**Why 1.8x not 2x for Tier 1:**  
BTC/ETH at 2x → DD = 20.2-20.4% → exactly at KILL threshold. 
1.8x gives 18.3-18.5% DD — 1.5pp buffer to KILL. Still delivers +40% return.

## Alpha Stack V2 Roadmap

### Implemented (V1)
- ✅ Asset-specific leverage tiers
- ✅ Risk Guard states (CL≥5 SOFT_PAUSE, DD>20% KILL)

### Next (Paper Trading Validation)
- ⬜ 2x Leverage Guard-trigger frequency measurement
- ⬜ Actual vs backtest exit reason comparison
- ⬜ Trailing stop realism validation (CLOSE vs real-time)

### Future (V2+)
- ⬜ Asset-Ranking: Top-N by 24h ROC when multiple signals fire
- ⬜ ADX-based position sizing: 0.5x (ADX 20-30), 1.0x (30-40), 1.5x (>40)
- ⬜ Limit-order engineering (Post-Only + 30s chase)
- ⬜ News/Macro Risk Layer (ADR-005 V2): KI score 0-100 → position sizing

### Explicitly Rejected
- ❌ ATR-based stops (tested, no improvement over ADX+EMA baseline)
- ❌ Kelly criterion (requires stable win-rate — unavailable in crypto)
- ❌ Extra HTF filter (already covered by ADX+EMA regime filter)
- ❌ 3x leverage (DD 29-51%, constant KILL triggers)

## Strategic Review Integration

backtest results are optimistic by ~69pp for BTC at 2% trailing with LOW-based 
checks. Paper trading with real-time WebSocket prices will determine the true 
exit behavior. If paper results significantly underperform backtest:
- Increase trailing from 2% → 3.5%
- Re-baseline with actual execution data
