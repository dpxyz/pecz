1. FUNDING RATE MECHANICS
- Hyperliquid uses SAME 8h formula as Binance: F = Pavg + clamp(interestRate - Pavg, -0.0005, 0.0005)
- BUT: settles every HOUR at 1/8 of the 8h rate → 24 settlement points/day vs Binance's 3
- Interest rate fixed at 0.01% per 8h (same as Binance)
- Premium index: 5-second sampling, based on impact bid/ask vs oracle spot
- Caps: 4% per hour (much looser than Binance's ±0.75×maintenance margin ratio)
- predictedFundings endpoint: returns predicted rates for HL + CEXs (Binance, Bybit)
- KEY DIFFERENCE: hourly time series vs sparse 8h series on Binance

2. FEE STRUCTURE
- Tier 0 (<5M USD 14d volume): Taker 0.045%, Maker 0.015%
- Higher tiers reduce fees progressively
- HYPE staking: up to 40% fee reduction (but uneconomical at our scale)
- Maker rebates: REAL but UNREACHABLE (need >0.5% global maker volume share → -0.001%, >1.5% → -0.002%, >3% → -0.003%)
- NO gas fees on trading, NO cancellation fees
- Deposit: USDC via Arbitrum One, no protocol fee (just L2 gas)
- Withdrawal: flat 1 USDC bridge fee
- No liquidation clearance fee (PnL goes to HLP/insurance)

COMPARISON (retail tier):
|            | Maker    | Taker    |
|------------|----------|----------|
| Hyperliquid| 0.015%   | 0.045%   |
| Binance    | 0.020%   | 0.050%   |

→ Maker on HL saves 0.005%/side vs Binance maker
→ If we can trade maker: 0.015% vs Binance taker 0.05% = 70% reduction in per-side fee drag

3. API & DATA QUALITY
- REST: 1200 weight/min/IP, standard requests weight=1, heavy calls up to 60
- WebSocket (wss://api.hyperliquid.xyz/ws): candle, l2Book, bbo, trades, activeAssetCtx, allDexsAssetCtxs, openOrders, orderUpdates, userFills, userEvents, userFundings
- MORE COMPLETE than Binance's mixed REST/WS model
- Historical candles: up to 5000 per request (1h = ~208 days)
- Historical funding: 500 rows/call (~20.8 days at hourly), paginated, NO global cutoff → can walk back full history
- OI: real-time via WebSocket, NO dedicated historical OI endpoint → need to self-archive or use Coinalyze/Coinglass
- Liquidations: WebSocket userEvents for own account, NO built-in hourly aggregation by long/short → must aggregate yourself
- Conclusion: clean APIs but need to self-archive for multi-year history

4. ASSET COVERAGE
- 200+ perp markets, 170+ crypto perps
- ALL our assets present: BTC-USD, ETH-USD, SOL-USD, AVAX-USD, DOGE-USD, ADA-USD
- Binance has broader overall coverage, but equivalent for majors-focused quant system

5. EXECUTION QUALITY
- SOL-USD: 24h volume hundreds of millions, OI 100M-1B, spreads 0.01-0.02%
- At €500-5k notional: <<1% of top-of-book depth, slippage dominated by price volatility not book shape
- Contract multiplier: 1, sizes in coin units, szDecimals for min increment
- Minimum order sizes well below our target range
- Stop-loss: Stop Market, Stop Limit, TP/SL supported as triggered market orders
- Edge cases: badTriggerPxRejected, marketOrderNoLiquidityRejected possible
- Comparable to Binance at our size, with caveat of HL being newer with at least one API outage

6. ACCOUNT & KYC
- Non-custodial DEX: wallet = account, no KYC required
- Terms prohibit US/sanctioned users, but no enforcement
- EU/Germany: technically accessible, but MiFID II still applies legally
- Deposit: USDC via Arbitrum One
- Withdrawal: 1 USDC flat fee, seconds to ~15 min

7. Z-SCORE RECALCULATION
- HL hourly rate f_h,t; equivalent 8h rate = 8 × f_h,t
- Option A: Resample HL to 8h (sum 8 hourly payments), compare directly with Binance
- Option B: Move entire stack to hourly for both venues (interpolate Binance's 8h rate)
- RECOMMENDED: Keep 7-day horizon, use 168 hourly samples (7×24) for z-score
  - Compute 8h-equivalent rates for both venues
  - Rolling mean + stddev on normalized 8h-equivalent series
  - This gives 8× more data points per window → statistically more robust z-scores
- Cross-venue comparison possible via predictedFundings endpoint (shows HL + Binance + Bybit rates)

8. RISKS & DOWNSIDES (from truncated section)
- Platform risk: DEX, smart contract risk (Arbitrum bridge, HyperCore contracts)
- JELLY incident: oracle override emergency mechanism
- Smart contract exploits: HyperVault, JELLY vaults multi-million losses
- Oct 2025: ~$10B liquidations, $1.23B trader capital wiped
- Regulatory grey zone: no KYC, blocks US but no enforcement for EU
- Binance has more mature support, fiat rails, longer track record
- Bottom line: better funding granularity + lower fees, but higher platform/smart-contract/governance risk
